Hello! I'm Magda - a designer and engineer based in London.

about me

I am a creative with background in design and engineering. I believe deeply that art and science are far more closely related than we realise most of the time and I am dedicated to exploring the intersections and crossovers of these two fields. I am passionate about design, technology, sustainability and challenging the status quo. I appreciate simplicity and accessibility in design and always strive for these in my work.

In 2016, I graduated from MA Design and Innovation course at Goldsmiths, University of London where I spent a year researching the possibilities of applying open-source principles to physical product design and proposed an innovative way of manufacturing laboratory equipment for scientific reseach. Before then, I studied BEng in Medical Engineering at Queen Mary, University of London. It was there, where I was first inroduced to design thinking principles and understood the importance of creativity in engineering. Currently, I work at Facebook, helping businesses to connect with their customers. Outside of work, I am a minimalist, vegan, runner and photography enthusiat. I am always on the lookout for new challenges and exciting projects to get involved in.

projects

Some examples of my previous work.

Open Source Product Design

My MA thesis at Goldsmiths, University of London. Exhibited at London Design Festival 2016. The project proposes open-source products, based on the open and collaborative design of a laboratory equipment for medical research. The syringe pump for microfluidics, as an example of using emerging technologies as force for good, proposes a vision of what product design could look like in the future. Read more

Sound & vision

Sound sensitive lights installation built with Arduino, sound sensors and LEDs. The LEDs light up to the sound of music and the light intensity is corresponding to the loudness of the sound. The project is based on the open-source code and is a prototype of what could be an interactive installation in a public space, engaging the audience in a multi-sensory experience. The lights express the sound of music in a way that could be experienced by deaf and the intense light has been proven to be sensed by blind people. Together, this installation provides an inclusive experience that could be enjoyed by more people than an ordinary concert. See more

Smart plant watering system

A smart plant watering system using Arduino and soil moisture sensors. The system automatically detects when humidity drops below required level and triggers watering the plants using pumps submerged in a tank filled with collected rainwater.
